About TVM

Law meets art.
We bridge the gap.

The Vie Mentorship is a structured, cycle-based mentorship programme for aspiring and early-stage entertainment lawyers across Nigeria and Africa.

We pair penultimate and final-year law students , and young lawyers within their first two years post-call , with experienced practitioners in Music and Film law, equipping them with the knowledge and networks to build meaningful careers at the intersection of creativity and law.

The Programme

What you'll learn

Each cycle is a deep-dive into the practical realities of entertainment law , from drafting contracts to understanding rights clearance and industry deal structures.

🎵
Music Law

Explore recording contracts, publishing agreements, royalties, sync licensing, neighbouring rights, and the evolving landscape of streaming and digital distribution.

Core Focus
🎬
Film & Screen Law

Navigate co-production deals, chain of title, clearances, talent agreements, distribution contracts, and financing structures in the African film industry.

Core Focus
⚖️
Practical Skills

Contract drafting, negotiation techniques, IP registration, dispute resolution, and career navigation , built for the realities of practice in Nigeria and across Africa.

How It Works

The TVM cycle

Applications Open

Each cycle's application window is announced in advance. Dates are fixed and publicised , watch this space or join the notification list.

Selection

Up to 50 mentees are selected per cycle. Applications are reviewed holistically , your enthusiasm and goals matter as much as your background.

The Programme

Structured sessions covering Music and Film law, led by practising entertainment lawyers. Mentorship, community, and real learning , together.

Certification

Graduates receive a verifiable digital certificate from The Vie Mentorship , a credential you can share, and anyone can verify instantly.
